## 1) One-Line Pitch

CryptFundIt is an India-first donation transparency platform that combines NITI-linked NGO verification, Gemini-powered fraud screening, and Algorand milestone escrow so donors can see exactly where money goes before full funds are released.

## 2) 30-Second Elevator Pitch

Donating should not feel like blind trust. In India, donors often struggle to verify NGO claims and track how funds are used after payment. CryptFundIt solves this with three layers of trust: NITI-aware verification of NGOs, Gemini-based evidence and fraud analysis, and milestone-based escrow on Algorand so money is released only when work is proven. Instead of one-time opaque donations, we create a transparent journey from donation to verified impact.

## 3) 90-Second Pitch Script

Problem: Donors want to help, but lack proof. Existing donation flows are often one-way transactions with limited post-donation transparency. NGOs may be genuine, but evidence is fragmented across PDFs, photos, websites, and social updates.

Opportunity: India has a massive social funding ecosystem. According to Bain and Dasra (India Philanthropy Report 2025), social sector funding reached approximately INR 25 lakh crore in FY2024 and is projected to reach approximately INR 45 lakh crore by FY2029. At the same time, the sector remains materially underfunded versus social needs, so trust infrastructure can improve capital flow quality.

Solution: CryptFundIt introduces milestone-locked giving. NGOs onboard with India-specific details and registry checks. Donors fund campaigns into Algorand escrow. NGOs must submit milestone evidence before payouts. Gemini analyzes documents and images, summarizes proof, and flags inconsistencies. Human reviewers approve or reject payout requests. Every release is linked to a milestone decision and an on-chain transaction.

Why now: NITI Darpan provides a large base for structured NGO identity checks, and modern multimodal AI can finally process mixed evidence formats at review speed.

Impact: We reduce misuse risk by limiting fund release blast radius, increase donor confidence with visible proof trails, and help legitimate NGOs prove work faster and more clearly.

## 8.1) Large Event Pre-Booking: How Escrow Still Works

Some NGO events need money upfront for venue booking, logistics, and deposits. CryptFundIt supports this with controlled advance milestones.

Two-step release model:

- Step A (Pre-event advance): release only a capped portion (for example 20-40%) after verifying pre-booking documents such as proforma invoices and booking contracts.
- Step B (Post-event settlement): release the remaining amount only after final completion proof is submitted and reviewed.

Protection controls:

- advance release caps by NGO risk tier
- dual approval for high-value advances
- Gemini compares planned vs final invoices and flags mismatches
- unresolved reconciliation auto-freezes future payouts

Pitch line:

We support real-world pre-funding needs without losing escrow discipline by splitting milestone release into verified advance and verified settlement.

## 14) Q&A Defense Bank

### Q: Why blockchain here?

A: We use blockchain for escrow state and payout traceability, not hype. Milestone release events become tamper-resistant and donor-visible.

### Q: Why Gemini and not rule-based checks only?

A: Rule checks catch format issues. Gemini handles unstructured, multimodal evidence and produces reviewer-readable summaries and fraud signals at operational speed.

### Q: Can AI approve payouts?

A: No. AI is advisory. Human reviewers approve milestone completion, and escrow release is gated.

### Q: What if NITI Darpan data is incomplete?

A: We treat registry matching as a trust input, not a guarantee. Missing or conflicting records trigger explicit manual review and higher scrutiny.

### Q: How do you prevent full fund drain?

A: Milestone-based escrow and payout caps ensure partial release only after verified progress.

### Q: What if an NGO needs funds before the event for bookings?

A: We use an advance-enabled milestone policy. A capped pre-event tranche is released only after verifying booking documents, and the balance is locked until post-event proof and invoice reconciliation are complete.
